Pro Se Guidebook

A system, not a guess.

Records win cases. Memory loses them.

Rule One: If it matters, document it. If it is not documented, expect it to be disputed.

01 · Before Court

Know your charge, deadline, hearing type, and what relief you are asking for before you walk in.

  • Bring printed copies.
  • Know your timeline cold.
  • Keep your request simple.

02 · Evidence Rules

Evidence needs context. A screenshot without date, source, and explanation is weaker than it should be.

  • Label every exhibit.
  • Track where it came from.
  • Explain why it matters.

03 · Motions

A motion should identify the problem, cite the support, and ask for a specific remedy.

  • Issue.
  • Facts.
  • Legal basis.
  • Relief requested.

04 · Deadlines

Deadlines are traps for the unorganized. Track every court date, filing date, response date, and appeal window.

  • Calendar it immediately.
  • Set reminders.
  • Keep proof of filing.

05 · Hearings

At a hearing, do not chase every rabbit. Lead with the cleanest issue and strongest proof.

  • Be brief.
  • Stay factual.
  • Ask clearly.

06 · Cross-Examination

Ask controlled questions. One fact per question. Do not argue with the witness.

  • Use short questions.
  • Use prior statements.
  • Stop when you get the point.

07 · Common Mistakes

Most people lose ground because their proof is scattered, not because they have no proof.

  • Do not submit messy packets.
  • Do not miss deadlines.
  • Do not rely on verbal promises.

08 · Emergency Checklist

When pressure hits, simplify. Facts first. Timeline second. Relief third.

  • What happened?
  • When did it happen?
  • Where is the proof?
  • What do you want the court to do?
